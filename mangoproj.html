As a cognitive science and computer science major at the University of Michigan, I'm constantly looking for projects that allow me to hone my programming skills while letting my creativity shine through. Please check out my favorites here!

Can't Sleep

My piece is an interactive sketch inspired by the final segment of holding death close by Brazilian artist enorê, which resonated with me through its haunting use of dialogue and exploration of sleep as a possible metaphor for death.

While enorê’s work removes agency from the viewer, I wanted to play with the illusion of choice using the familiar language of video game prompts. The user faces a looping purgatory of unsettling messages—no choice brings relief.

Languages Used: Javascript, HTML

NextChapter

NextChapter is a social platform that connects readers with book clubs tailored to their interests and reading pace. It focuses on community, not just book discovery, enabling users to track progress and engage in shared discussions.

Built with React Native/Expo, Firebase, and the Google Books API, the app helps users stay motivated to finish books and share reflections with others.

Languages Used: Javascript

Elderly Monitoring - Machine Learning

This project explores non-intrusive elderly monitoring using environmental gas sensor data. I built machine learning models to detect which room a person is in—using only CO₂ and similar data—without needing cameras or wearables.

The result showed strong accuracy and potential for passive, privacy-preserving elder care.

Languages Used: Python
